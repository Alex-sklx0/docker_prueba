Band History

The Smashing Pumpkins formed in Chicago in 1988 by frontman Billy Corgan and guitarist James Iha. Bassist D'arcy Wretzky and drummer Jimmy Chamberlin completed the original lineup.

After achieving mainstream success in the 1990s with albums like Siamese Dream and Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness, the band went through several lineup changes before breaking up in 2000.

Billy Corgan revived the band in 2006 with a new lineup, and in 2018, the original members (except D'arcy Wretzky) reunited for the Shiny and Oh So Bright tour.

To date, The Smashing Pumpkins have released 11 studio albums and sold over 30 million records worldwide, making them one of the most commercially successful and influential alternative rock bands of all time.
